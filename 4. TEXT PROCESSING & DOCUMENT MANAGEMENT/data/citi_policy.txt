CITI GLOBAL TREASURY RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY
Document Ref: TR-2026-LQ-04
Division: Investment Banking and Capital Markets
Classification: Internal Use Only
================================================================================

[SECTION 1: OVERVIEW & DIVISION RESPONSIBILITIES]
The Corporate Treasury division, operating closely with Investment Banking and 
Capital Markets, maintains structural oversight of the bank's balance sheet balance. 
It is the explicit mandate of this division to identify, measure, monitor, and control 
liquidity risks across all global business units and legal entities.

[SECTION 2: DEFINITION OF LIQUIDITY RISK]
Liquidity Risk is defined as the risk that Citi will be unable to efficiently meet 
both expected and unexpected current and future cash flows and collateral needs 
without adversely affecting either daily operations or the financial condition of 
the firm. This includes the inability to manage sudden spikes in customer withdrawals, 
unanticipated systemic market disruptions, or wholesale funding freezes.

[SECTION 3: BUFFER & MITIGATION PROTOCOLS]
To mitigate structural Liquidity Risk, Citi's Capital Markets team enforces the 
strict maintenance of a High-Quality Liquid Asset (HQLA) buffer. This buffer 
consists exclusively of unencumbered cash, central bank reserves, and sovereign 
securities that can be instantly liquidated in a severe stress scenario. 

[SECTION 4: COMPLIANCE METRICS]
All regional desks must report their Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) daily to the 
Investment Banking and Capital Markets risk committee. The LCR must consistently 
exceed 110%, ensuring that the bank holds a sufficient cushion of HQLA to survive 
a 30-day acute stress period.
